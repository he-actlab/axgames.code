Game#1 - Pollice Verso

Pollice Verso is a betting game that you guess other players' consensus on how much quality is acceptable. One game constitutes {{ max_round }} rounds. Initially, you are given a certain amount of balance, ${{ GAME1_INIT_BALANCE }}, and the higher balance you have at the end of the game, the higher rewards you will achieve. As the game starts, you will see two outputs generated by an approximate computing application, {{ KERNEL_NAME }}: (1) original output (2) approximate output. You can now guess what other players have thought of the acceptability of the approximate output. The amount of betting money will reflects how much you are confident on your guess.



Game#2 - WinABatt

WinABatt is another game that you guess how other players think on the output quality. You are given an image that initially has 50% quality degradation. You can slide the knob to improve the quality and it requires more cost. The reward increases in proportion to how close your choice of quality is to the point that others consensus changes from "good enough" to "unacceptable". Hence, your goal should be to find a quality as close as possible to the point that maximizes the reward and as low as possible that minimizes the cost.



Game#3 - QnA

QnA is a game, mostly similar to WinABatt, but it differs in that you will be given one question regarding the output. Other players will try to find a quality that is required to get the correct answer for the given question. Again your reward proportionally increases according to how close your choice of quality is to others'. For an incorrect answer, you will get a penalty, which in turn decrease your reward.
