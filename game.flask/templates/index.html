Instructions
Game#1 - Pollice Verso

Pollice Verso is a betting game that you guess other players' consensus on how much quality is acceptable. One game constitutes {{ max_round }} rounds. Initially, you are given a certain amount of balance, ${{ GAME1_INIT_BALANCE }}, and the higher balance you have at the end of the game, the higher rewards you will achieve. As the game starts, you will see two {{ KERNEL_NAME }} output images: (1) original output (2) approximate output. You can now guess what other players have thought of the acceptability of the approximate output. The amount of betting money will reflects how much you are confident on your guess.



Game#2 - WinABatt

WinABatt is another game that you guess how other players think on the quality of an image. You are given an image that initially has 50% quality degradation. You can slide the knob to improve the quality and it requires more cost. The reward increases in proportion to how close your choice of quality is to the point that others consensus changes from "good enough" to "unacceptable". Hence, your goal should be to find a quality as close as possible to the point that maximizes the reward and as low as possible that minimizes the cost.



Game#3 - QnA

QnA is a game, mostly similar to WinABatt, but it differs in that you will be given one question regarding the image. Other players will try to find a quality that is required to get the correct answer for the given question. Again your reward proportionally increases according to how close your choice of quality is to others'. For an incorrect answer, you will get a penalty, which in turn decrease your reward.

CONSENT DOCUMENT FOR ENROLLING ADULT PARTICIPANTS IN A RESEARCH STUDY

Georgia Institute of Technology
Project Title: Playing a Game to Measure Acceptable Quality Loss from Approximate Computing

Investigators: Hadi Esmaeilzadeh, Jongse Park
Protocol and Consent Title: Main23/03/15v1

You are being asked to be a volunteer in a research study.

Purpose:
The purpose of this study is to evaluate how much quality degradation due to an approximate computing kernel (Edge Detection: Sobel filter) is acceptable by application users. We expect to enroll up to 100 people in this study.

Exclusion/Inclusion Criteria:
This game is very intuitive and does not require any expertise in any academic domains. However, since the subjects should be able to understand the instructions and play the game, people who cannot read and understand English are not eligible for this study. A person who is under 18 is not eligible.

Procedures:
The game has been implemented as a web game so every subject can just access using easily downloadable web browsers such as Google Chrome, Firefox, and MS Internet explorer.

The instructions for the games are given in this page on the left bottom side. Please read the instructions carefully and play the game.

You will play all the three games once respectively. Each game will take less than 5~10 minutes so in total, the study will be finished within at most 30 minutes. However, you can play the three games as many times as you want. Risks or Discomforts:
The risks involved are no greater than those involved in daily activities such as playing other web games.

Benefits:
You are not likely to benefit in any way from joining this study. We hope that what we learn will someday help improve the research in approximate computing.

Compensation to You:
None.

Confidentiality:
The following procedures will be followed to keep your personal information confidential in this study: The data collected about you will be kept private to the extent allowed by law. To protect your privacy, your records will be kept under a code number rather than by name. Your records will be kept in locked files and only study staff will be allowed to look at them. Your name and any other fact that might point to you will not appear when results of this study are presented or published. Your privacy will be protected to the extent allowed by law. To make sure that this research is being carried out in the proper way, the Georgia Institute of Technology IRB may review study records. The Office of Human Research Protections may also look over study records during required reviews.

Costs to You:
There are no costs to you, other than your time, for being in this study.

Neither the Principal Investigator nor Georgia Institute of Technology has made provision for payment of costs associated with any injury resulting from participation in this study.

  • You have the right to change your mind and leave the study at any time without giving any reason and without penalty.
  • Any new information that may make you change your mind about being in this study will be given to you.
